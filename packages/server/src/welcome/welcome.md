# Start here

This page is an artifact. Somebody's assistant wrote a document, published it,
and got back a link. That is the whole product, and you are looking at the
result of it.

It is yours. It is private until you share it, and you can delete it whenever
you like.

## Try leaving a comment

Select any sentence on this page and a comment button appears.

<!-- ANCHOR -->
There is already a comment on this page. Open it and reply to it.

That is what makes this different from sending someone a file. A comment is
attached to the exact words it is about, so nobody has to write "in the third
paragraph under the second heading" ever again.

When you republish a document, comments stay attached to the text they were
about. If that text is gone, the comment says so instead of quietly moving
somewhere it does not belong. A comment that pretends to still be about
something is worse than one that admits it lost its place.

## What to say to your assistant

You do not run commands. You say what you want, and the assistant does it.

To publish something it just wrote:

> Publish that as an artifact and give me the link.

To send it to somebody:

> Share it with priya@example.com.

To pick up feedback and act on it:

> Check the comments on that doc and update it.

That last one is the loop worth knowing. People comment, your assistant reads
the comments, changes the document, and republishes it. The link never changes,
so anybody holding it sees the new version.

## Sharing somebody in is how they get an account

When you share a document with an address that has never been here before, that
person gets an email and an account is created for them when they open it. There
is no invitation to send separately and nothing for them to install.

So if you want a colleague on this, share something with them. That is the
invitation.

## When this is useful

Two moments, mostly:

You want to send a document to somebody and you do not want to attach a file.
Files get downloaded, renamed, edited by one person, and emailed back as
version 4 final. A link stays one document.

You want to read what your assistant wrote without opening a file on your
laptop. A report with headings, tables and highlighted code is easier to read as
a page than as raw text in a terminal.

## What to do with this page

Nothing, if you like. Delete it once you have had a look.

If you want to try the whole loop first, reply to the comment on this page and
then tell your assistant to check the comments on it. That takes about a minute
and shows you everything at once.
